### Q: Can I use DCYFR for free?

**A:** Yes! DCYFR is free for:
- Personal projects (no revenue)
- Educational use (students, teachers, coursework)
- Open source projects (non-commercial)
- Evaluation and testing (pre-production)

### Q: What counts as "commercial use"?

**A:** Commercial use means using DCYFR in a context that generates revenue or is business-related:

**Examples of commercial use:**
- Building a SaaS product that generates revenue
- Using DCYFR at a company with >5 employees
- Providing paid consulting/development services using DCYFR
- Distributing DCYFR as part of a commercial product

**Not commercial use:**
- Personal side projects (no revenue)
- Educational projects (students, teachers)
- Open source projects (non-commercial)
- Internal tools at small startups (<5 employees, <$1M revenue)

**A:** Yes, **Developer tier or higher** is required.

## Compliance

### Q: How do you enforce licensing?

**A:**

1. **Monitor** for commercial use (GitHub stars, npm downloads, web scraping)
2. **Contact** users with friendly outreach
3. **Educate** about licensing model
4. **Offer** licensing options with grace period
5. **Legal action** if necessary (rare, last resort)

**Philosophy:** We prefer to work with you to get compliant rather than pursue legal action.

### Q: What if I'm accidentally out of compliance?

**A:** Contact licensing@dcyfr.ai immediately.

We understand accidental violations happen. We'll work with you to:
- Understand your use case
- Find the right licensing tier
- Provide a grace period for compliance
- Avoid legal action if you act in good faith

**Most violations are accidentalâ€”we're here to help, not punish.**

### Q: Can you audit my use of DCYFR?

**A:** Commercial licenses include audit rights (with reasonable notice).

We reserve the right to verify compliance for commercial licenses, but we'll:
- Provide advance notice (30+ days)
- Work around your schedule
- Keep proprietary information confidential

**Note:** This is rare and typically only done for Enterprise tier customers.
